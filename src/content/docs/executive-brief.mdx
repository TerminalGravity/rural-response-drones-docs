## The opportunity

Rural recreation regions like Lake Havasu / Mohave County face a hard emergency-response problem: extreme heat, desert terrain, OHV incidents, boating accidents, remote trails, limited cellular coverage, and long search areas.

Drones are useful because they can reduce search time, improve responder safety, provide thermal overwatch, map search grids, and give incident command better situational awareness.

## The market reality

The buyer is not the general public. The buyer is the agency that owns public safety operations:

- Mohave County Sheriff / SAR
- Mohave County Emergency Management
- Lake Havasu fire / police / marine patrol
- rural fire districts
- EMS agencies
- tribal public safety
- parks / land managers

Arizona county SAR responsibility sits with the sheriff. Mohave County already has volunteer SAR and public-facing drone operations. The viable startup therefore supports the official system; it does not replace it.

## Current verdict

<div class="decision-box">
<strong>Go, but only as a service/integration wedge.</strong> Build a rural public-safety drone enablement company: grant support, program design, FAA/SOP/policy package, training, maintenance, exercises, and optional on-call surge support.
</div>

## The first sellable product

**Grant-Ready Drone SAR Program Package** — $15K–$25K planning product.

Deliverables:

- needs assessment
- agency-owned drone program design
- grant target list
- grant narrative and budget
- equipment specification
- FAA / Part 107 / COA readiness checklist
- SOP + privacy + data-retention templates
- training plan
- 90-day pilot plan
- board/council-ready concept note

## The implementation product

**Rural UAS Readiness Implementation** — $50K–$150K depending on grant size.

Deliverables:

- procurement support
- drone setup
- pilot training
- monthly drills
- maintenance system
- battery and flight log procedures
- incident command workflow
- grant compliance reporting

## The recurring product

**Readiness Retainer** — $1.5K–$5K/month.

Includes:

- recurrent training
- quarterly exercises
- firmware/security updates
- battery lifecycle checks
- flight log review
- annual grant support
- standby/surge pilot hours

## Next decision

Do not buy equipment yet. First validate with public-safety conversations and one agency willing to sponsor or co-develop a pilot proposal.
